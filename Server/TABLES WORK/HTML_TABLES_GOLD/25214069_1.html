To examine this relationship further, we divided the patients by rate of bone loss at the total hip during P3 into tertiles (Fig. 1). We found that the patients in the tertile with the greatest rates of bone loss during P3 had the greatest rates of bone gain during Z3; conversely, the patients in the tertile with the lowest rates of bone loss during P3 had the lowest rates of bone gain during Z3. For example, those in the tertile with greatest loss (2.3%) during the placebo period gained the most (3.1%) during subsequent ZOL treatment. To test whether this last result was robust, we repeated the analysis in the subset with change in total hip BMD during P3 and change in hip BMD between 3 and 6 years (rather than 3 to 4.5 years) (r ¼ –0.24, p < 0.0001, n ¼ 500). To address the common variable effect, we repeated the analysis with change in hip BMD during the ﬁrst 2 years of P3 and change in hip BMD from year 3 to 4.5 years (r ¼ –0.09, p ¼ 0.0049, n ¼ 1023).
