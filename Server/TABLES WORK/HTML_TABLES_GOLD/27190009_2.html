Figure 2 Mean change in total UPDRS scores adjusted according to baseline UPDRS at 12, 24, 36 and 48 weeks stratiﬁed according to genotype at rs2283265 and treatment group.For visualization of the treatment (i.e. rasagiline) and placebo-controlled data, we compared mean change in total UPDRS from baseline at 12, 24, and 36 weeks in those with two C alleles (i.e. CC genotype) to those with either one C allele or no C allele indicated by A* (i.e. CA or AA genotypes are binned together). The rationale for collapsing genotypic groups is based on the small number of individuals with the AA genotype (n = 14 placebo, n = 9 treated); for CC genotype: n = 226 placebo and n = 220 treated; for CA + AA genotype: n = 93 placebo and n = 96 treated. Week 0 represents the mean change baseline values, that is, 0 as the baseline measure is taken at this time. As evidenced by the plots, treated carriers of the A allele did not only show a lack of initial improve-ment in Parkinson’s disease symptoms, but they also took longer for a drug effect to be seen with clinical stabilization being achieved by 24 weeks. Week 48 data are also included for only the delayed start group indicating that those with the CC genotype were consistently less symptomatic at Weeks 36 and 48 than those with the CA + AA genotypes
