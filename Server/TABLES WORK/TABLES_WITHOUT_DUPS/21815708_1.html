Incidence of cardiovascular disease by age in all patients and in men and women during follow-up.The hazard ratio (HR) and 95% confidence interval (CI) were estimated using the Cox proportional hazards model adjusted for hypertension, diabetes mellitus and high- density lipoprotein cholesterol level. The significant difference between pravastatin plus diet and diet alone was found both in all populations and in >50 women, whereas, in men, the difference was significant only in all men. Squares indicate the number of events in each group; horizontal lines are 95% CIs. The overall total HR is represented as a diamond, in the centre of which is an estimate of HR, with the 95% CI indicated by the width of the diamond. NNT=number needed to treat; *p<0.05, **p<0.01 for diet plus pravastatin vs diet alone.
